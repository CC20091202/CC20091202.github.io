Poem About Me

Growing up I thought people were born with their heads cocked, because that's how they always looked at me, a girl in a foreign country. Growing up I thought people were born wh their heads cocked, because that's how they always looked at me, a girl in a foreign country.

Boxes, check one, check other.

People don't know, they don't furrow between the layers like I do.

They don't switch and flick and actively make the decisions of which...

Which part of me belongs today?

Which aspect of my personality with offend the least and blend the most, and work and succeed and bury the lead like a switchboard of traits that decided my fate and I'm always the imposter.

Always lost, always asking for directions, and people point my way like a scarecrow, like tornadoes blowing me whichever way the wind blows.

Well, Dorothy doesn't want to play today, she's becoming the machiavellian because she cares.

The box is empty, and glaring and daring me to choose one.

Well, I'm an expert at boxes, my whole life can fit in just about one, and I've got it down to a science.

I can pack my entire identity in an hour, 'cause where there's roots there's power.

But, I am all topsoil. My blood runs like oil and water refusing to stick.

Those secret library books, read in old library nooks.  

The phone camera that locks all my memories in a flash, saved for when my recollection doesn't last.

That computer that sparked that fire and the memories of those passwords, are all ready in a box to be carried door to door

But that's never the kind of box anyone ever asks for.
